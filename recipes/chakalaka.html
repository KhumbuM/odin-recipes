Chakalaka

Description

Chakalaka is a traditional South African dish believed to have started by migrant labourers working at the mines in Johannesburg. The men will mix several vegetables together together with chillies and enjoy it with their choice of starch

It is enjoyed all over the land and it always takes place of honour at many a braai table over the weekend while people are enjoying watching their favourite sports team play on television

Ingridients

  • 1/4 cup cooking oil
  • 1 onion diced
  • 1 to 2 tomatoes
  • 3 to 4 cups of sliced cabbage
  • 1 large carrot grated
  • 1 green pepper diced
  • 1 red pepper diced
  • 1 tin of baked beans
  • 1 tablespoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 tablespoon of paprika

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a large saucepan over medium heat
  2. Add onion
  3. Stir in all spices then add tomatoes, peppers, carrots and cabbage
  4. Simmer for about 5 minutes while stirring occasionally
  5. Add baked beans and continue cooking for another 2 to 3 minutes
  6. Adjust for seasong and serve warm
